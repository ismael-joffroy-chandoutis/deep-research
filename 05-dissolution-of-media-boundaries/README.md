# AI and the Dissolution of Media Boundaries: Inhabiting Generated Worlds

*© Ismaël Joffroy Chandoutis*

---

At the moment when **Decart** unveils *Oasis*, the first AI model capable of generating a playable virtual world in real time without a game engine, and when **Runway** enriches its *Gen-3 Alpha Turbo* with camera controls that allow free navigation through generated spaces, we are witnessing a major bifurcation in the evolution of media. These advances foreshadow the possibility of creating entire worlds from a single prompt. Two paths take shape: on one side, the industrialization of creation with the promise of a "generative Netflix" where every piece of content would be produced on demand; on the other, the emergence of an artistic practice that sees in these tools the opportunity for a radical reconfiguration of our relationship to the real.

---

## Beyond the production chains

My trajectory as an artist and filmmaker led me, as early as 2006, to explore game engines as tools for creation, seeking to liquefy the boundaries between writing, shooting, and editing. Unity and Unreal have already transformed film production; AI today marks a deeper rupture: the possibility of generating entire worlds in real time, without any apparent technical mediation.

---

## Drilling into memetic strata

My post-documentary practice explores the possibilities generative AI offers to reconfigure our relationship to archives. I train my models not only on images but also on sounds, creating a dialogue between past and present. Archives are no longer frozen documents but living matter that feeds the generation of new forms of expression.

---

## Latent space as territory of exploration

This approach transcends simple technical convergence. In these generated spaces, narration becomes fluid, perpetually modulable. A single parameter can radically transform the visual universe or the narrative point of view. It is no longer a question of representation but of active exploration of new forms of existence.

---

## Play as creative methodology

My approach draws from the mechanics of video games but diverts them from their primary function. I am looking for that particular state where interaction becomes intuitive, where tools fade away to leave room for a spontaneous flow of creation. This is what I call "artistic gameplay": a way of being in the creative instant, like a player who forgets their controls to merge entirely with the experience.

This approach radically transforms the creation process. Instead of following the traditional stages (writing, shooting, editing), I navigate freely between these moments, letting them respond to and influence one another. AI is not a simple tool at my disposal, but a collaborator that can surprise me, take me toward territories I had not anticipated. This share of the unpredictable, far from being an obstacle, becomes a source of discoveries and new experiments.

---

## The hyper-immediacy of generated worlds

This mutation goes beyond the simple remediation described by Bolter and Grusin: we are entering the era of hyper-immediacy, where images are generated and transformed instantly, abolishing the boundaries between media. Traditional distinctions fade in favor of a unified and dynamic experience.

---

These evolutions sketch the contours of an unprecedented artistic practice where creation no longer aims at reproducing the real but at actively exploring it. A practice that, rather than yielding to the sirens of automation and mass personalization, opens the way to new forms of sensible experience.
