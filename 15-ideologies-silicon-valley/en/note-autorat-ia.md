# Method note

> On the writing of this dossier, and the instrument I wrote it with. June 2026.

I wrote this dossier the way I work on my films: with instruments. Here the main instrument is a language model, Claude, made by Anthropic. I say so up front because, in a dossier whose subject is precisely the AI industry, that choice is not neutral, and it is part of the object.

## The instrument is in frame

Anthropic is not a secondary actor in this dossier: one part is devoted entirely to it, another dissects its CEO, a third contrasts its red lines with what its competitors sell. To write about this industry with one of its products is to put the instrument inside the frame of what it documents. For my practice that is the point of interest, not the flaw to hide: the instrument is part of the image. A dossier on the dissolution of the border between the human and the machine, written as if that border still held, would be lying about its own making.

## What that implies, without minimizing it

Three limits, which I take on rather than conceal.

1. **The conflict of interest.** The instrument can soften what bothers its maker, or conversely overplay severity to appear honest. The bias cuts both ways, and it is structural. It is mine to watch, part by part.
2. **Epistemic dependence.** The model is trained on the very corpus the dossier analyzes: the tech press, the manifestos, the forums, the essays of this milieu. That discourse is not read from an outside, it is partly the fabric of the instrument. There is no position of pure exteriority, and no one has one.
3. **The absence of an outside.** Where a journalist can claim some distance, here the tool is inside. I do not erase it: I use it, and I remain responsible for what is kept, set aside, framed.

## The method, and its safeguards

What holds the dossier together in spite of this, and is its real subject:

- **The research is delegated to agents, then adversarially verified**, claim by claim: each load-bearing assertion is submitted to a counter-research designed to refute it, and the corrections are recorded in the method notes of each part.
- **Everything is sourced** with links, and **the actors' denials are included** in the body of the text, not set aside.
- **The favorable and unfavorable elements** for each actor sit side by side, including for Anthropic.

These safeguards reduce factual error. They do not remove framing bias: the choice of what deserves a part, the tone, the selection of quotations, the decision to use the word "fascism." Those decisions are mine. Read this dossier as situated work, not as neutral speech from above.

## What I sign

I am not signing a text I produced alone, nor a text the machine produced in my place. I sign a direction: what was searched for, what was kept, what was set aside, and the form it all took. The instrument writes a great deal; I decide what it means.

*Ismaël Joffroy Chandoutis*
